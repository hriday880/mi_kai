## Session Context
First session with the user. Goal: plan and begin building the Mi-KAI Tokyo ultra-luxury LED brand website.

## Reasoning: Brand Analysis

When I examined the box images, several key observations shaped my design thinking:

1. **The box is the brand.** Matte black with gold foil stamping — this is unmistakably luxury. The website must mirror this tactile quality digitally. Every surface should feel like dark matte paper, every accent should feel like pressed gold foil.

2. **The Kanji 光明 (Kōmyō)** means "radiance" or "brilliant light" — this is deeply intentional for an LED brand. It carries Buddhist connotations of enlightenment and illumination. This should subtly inform the website's narrative: light as philosophy, not just product.

3. **The logo typeface** is geometric, blocky, and has Kanji-inspired forms (the "m" in "mi" looks like it could be a Japanese character). This hybrid identity (Japanese precision + global design) is the brand's DNA.

4. **CRI>90** is prominently featured — this is a technical differentiator. The "Experience" section should make CRI understandable and aspirational for non-technical visitors.

5. **Product positioning**: ₹1280 MRP for an 18W surface light — this is premium but accessible luxury. The website tone should be aspirational but not exclusionary.

## Reasoning: Navigation Architecture

The user said "mix of single scrollable and multipage based on your judgement." My thinking:

- **Why scroll for the main sections?** These 4 sections (House, Experience, Craft, Living) tell a sequential story: Who we are → Why our light matters → How we achieve it → How it transforms your life. Breaking this into separate pages would fracture the narrative. Scrolling creates a cinematic, directed experience — like opening a luxury brochure.

- **Why separate the contact page?** Contact is transactional, not narrative. Mixing a messaging form into the brand story would cheapen both. A dedicated `/contact` route with its own atmosphere (perhaps slightly lighter, more welcoming) preserves the story's integrity.

- **Why gate with the intro?** The box-opening animation is a first-impression moment. It sets the tone. After it plays, the user should never see it again in the same session (sessionStorage flag). This prevents annoyance on navigation.

## Reasoning: Typography Choices

- **Cormorant Garamond** for EN headings: It's one of the most elegant serif fonts available on Google Fonts. High contrast strokes, old-style proportions — it whispers luxury. It pairs beautifully with the bold geometric logo without competing.

- **Outfit** for EN body: Clean geometric sans-serif with excellent readability. Modern without being cold. The slight roundness adds warmth that complements the gold palette.

- **Shippori Mincho** for JP: This is a traditional Mincho (serif) typeface with refined strokes. It's the Japanese equivalent of Cormorant Garamond in spirit — elegant, traditional, premium.

- **Noto Serif SC** for CN: Google's Noto family provides excellent CJK coverage. The Serif variant harmonizes with Shippori Mincho while being optimized for Simplified Chinese.

## Reasoning: Tagline Direction

For the taglines I will draft, my thinking is:
- **Primary tagline**: Should evoke the philosophy of light, not just the product. Think "Where darkness meets design" or "Light, perfected."
- **Section taglines**: Each section gets its own sub-tagline that connects to the overarching philosophy.
- **Bilingual impact**: Using English + Japanese/Chinese side by side creates an immediate sense of international luxury (like how Issey Miyake or Shiseido present themselves).

## Reasoning: 3D Box Animation Architecture

The intro animation uses a CSS 3D cuboid built from 6 actual product box face images. Key technical decisions:

1. **Two-layer hinge pattern**: GSAP replaces entire CSS `transform` properties when animating. If an element has `transform: translateZ(-160px)` for positioning and GSAP sets `rotationX`, the translateZ gets wiped and the element flies off into space. The fix: split the lid into `hingePosition` (holds translateZ, GSAP never touches) and `hingePivot` (holds rotateX, GSAP animates). This separation of concerns ensures positioning and animation can't conflict.

2. **Cuboid proportions**: The physical box is 16×16×6 cm. Mapped to 320×320×120 px in CSS 3D space. Side faces are 320×120, top/bottom are 320×320. Half-depth = 160px for side translateZ, half-height = 60px for top/bottom translateZ.

3. **Hinge rotation direction**: The lid has `transform-origin: top center` and starts at `rotateX(90deg)` (closed/horizontal). To open: animate to `rotateX(-20deg)` — the front edge swings UP and over past vertical. Going from 90→0 = open 90°, from 90→-20 = open 110° past vertical, like a real box lid.

4. **Dive-in transition**: After the lid opens, the entire cube scales up 14x and shifts down (y: 600), creating the illusion of the camera flying through the open top into the box. Dark background panels split apart vertically to reveal the website underneath. The CTA text "Enter The House of Mi-KAI" reinforces this narrative of physical entry.
